CBSE Class 9 English Half Yearly Examination

Time: 3 Hours | Maximum Marks: 80

Section A: Reading (20 marks)

Q1. Unseen Passage (10 marks)

Read the following passage and answer the questions that follow:

Modern technology has transformed the world, especially in terms of communication. While face-to-face interactions remain vital, digital platforms are bridging gaps and bringing people together from different parts of the world. However, this increasing reliance on technology has also led to several challenges, such as a reduction in personal interactions and the rise of cyberbullying.

  1. What transformation has modern technology brought to communication? (2 marks)
  2. Mention two challenges faced due to the increased use of technology. (2 marks)
  3. How do digital platforms help in communication? (2 marks)
  4. Find a synonym for 'vital' from the passage. (1 mark)
  5. What is the title of the passage? (1 mark)
  6. Give two reasons why face-to-face interactions are important. (2 marks)

Q2. Unseen Passage (10 marks)

Read the following passage and answer the questions that follow:

The Himalayas, one of the youngest mountain ranges in the world, is not just a geographical feature but a vital part of India's cultural heritage. Stretching across several countries, they are home to a rich variety of flora and fauna. The Himalayas also play a crucial role in regulating the climate of the Indian subcontinent.

  1. Which mountain range is described in the passage? (1 mark)
  2. What role do the Himalayas play in India’s climate? (2 marks)
  3. How are the Himalayas a part of India's cultural heritage? (2 marks)
  4. What does the term ‘flora and fauna’ mean? (1 mark)
  5. Why are the Himalayas important for biodiversity? (2 marks)
  6. Find a word in the passage that means 'essential.' (1 mark)
  7. Name the countries through which the Himalayas extend. (1 mark)

Section C: Grammar (20 marks)

Q4. Grammar

a. Tense (5 marks)

Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb given in brackets:

  1. She always ____ (go) to school by bus.
  2. By the time you arrive, I ____ (complete) my work.
  3. The children ____ (play) outside when it started raining.
  4. We ____ (visit) our grandparents last summer.
  5. I ____ (study) English for five years.

b. Modals (5 marks)

Fill in the blanks using appropriate modals (can, may, must, should, could):

  1. You ____ wear a helmet while riding a bike.
  2. ____ I borrow your pen, please?
  3. They ____ be here by now, but they are late.
  4. You ____ see a doctor if you're not feeling well.
  5. We ____ finish the project by tomorrow.

Section D: Literature (20 marks)

Q5. Extracts

a. From “The Lost Child” (5 marks)

Read the extract below and answer the questions:

"The child turned his face from the toys and walked on. He knew he couldn’t have them, so he turned his mind to other things."

  1. Why did the child turn his face away from the toys?
  2. What does this tell us about the child’s understanding?
  3. Where was the child going?
  4. How does this moment contribute to the overall theme of the story?
  5. Who is the author of "The Lost Child"?

b. From “Wind” (Poem) (5 marks)

Read the following lines and answer the questions:

"Wind, come softly Don’t break the shutters of the windows Don’t scatter the papers."

  1. Who is the poet addressing in the poem?
  2. What does the poet request the wind to do?
  3. What effect does the wind have on the surroundings?
  4. Identify the poetic device in "scatter the papers."
  5. Who is the poet of this poem?

c. From “The Adventures of Toto” (5 marks)

Read the extract below and answer the questions:

"Toto was a pretty monkey. His bright eyes sparkled with mischief beneath deep-set eyebrows, and his teeth, which were a pearly white, were often displayed in a smile that frightened the life out of elderly Anglo-Indian ladies."

  1. How is Toto described in the extract?
  2. Why did Toto frighten the elderly Anglo-Indian ladies?
  3. What does the phrase "bright eyes sparkled with mischief" imply about Toto’s character?
  4. What is the tone of the author in this extract?
  5. Who is the author of “The Adventures of Toto”?
